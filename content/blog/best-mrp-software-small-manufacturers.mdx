## What Small Manufacturers Actually Need from MRP

Before diving into specific products, let us define what small manufacturers (under 100 employees) actually need versus what they get sold:

### Must-Have Features

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Bill of Materials management | Cannot do MRP without accurate BOMs |
| Inventory tracking | Real-time visibility of on-hand stock |
| Purchase order generation | Automated procurement based on MRP calculations |
| [Net requirements calculation](/blog/mrp-net-requirements-calculation) | Core MRP engine that determines what to order and when |
| Production order management | Track work orders through the shop floor |
| Basic reporting | Inventory status, order status, material shortages |

### High-Value Features

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| [Finite capacity scheduling](/blog/finite-capacity-planning-guide) | Plans production against real capacity constraints |
| Visual Gantt charts | Intuitive view of production schedule |
| [Lot sizing options](/blog/mrp-lot-sizing-methods) | Optimizes order quantities beyond lot-for-lot |
| [Safety stock management](/blog/safety-stock-mrp) | Prevents stockouts for critical items |
| What-if scenario planning | Test impact of changes before committing |
| ERP integration | Connects to your existing business systems |

### Features Most Small Manufacturers Do NOT Need

- Multi-currency and multi-language support
- Global supply chain management
- Advanced HR and payroll
- CRM with marketing automation
- Compliance modules for industries you are not in

Paying for these features increases cost and complexity without delivering value. Focus on tools built for manufacturing planning, not enterprise everything.

## How to Evaluate MRP Software: A Practical Framework

### Step 1: Define Your Requirements

Before contacting vendors, document your specific needs:

- How many active BOMs do you manage?
- What is your production model? (Make-to-order, make-to-stock, engineer-to-order, mixed)
- How many work centers or machines do you schedule across?
- What existing systems must the MRP tool integrate with?
- What is your biggest current pain? (Material shortages, late deliveries, capacity bottlenecks, inventory excess)

### Step 2: Calculate Total Cost of Ownership

Do not compare sticker prices. Compare 5-year total cost of ownership:

| Cost Component | Subscription Model (Example) | One-Time License (Example) |
|---|---|---|
| Year 1 license/subscription | $12,000 ($100/user/mo x 10 users) | $25,000 one-time |
| Year 1 implementation | $5,000 | $5,000 |
| Years 2-5 subscription | $48,000 ($12,000/yr x 4) | $0 |
| Years 2-5 maintenance | Included | $5,000 ($1,250/yr) |
| **5-Year Total** | **$65,000** | **$35,000** |

The one-time license model, which [RMDB](/resource-manager-db-2) uses, often delivers significantly lower total cost, especially as you add users over time.

### Step 3: Test with Your Actual Data

Never buy MRP software based on a demo with sample data. Provide vendors with your actual BOMs, inventory data, and a realistic set of production orders. Watch how the system handles your complexity, not a simplified demonstration.

### Step 4: Evaluate Implementation Support

Ask specific questions about implementation:

- Who does the data migration?
- How much training is included?
- What happens if we need help after go-live?
- What is the typical time from purchase to productive use?

Refer to our [MRP implementation checklist](/blog/mrp-implementation-checklist) for a complete list of evaluation criteria and go-live requirements.

### Step 5: Check References in Your Industry

Ask for references from manufacturers similar to your size, industry, and production model. A tool that works brilliantly for a consumer goods batch manufacturer may struggle in a high-mix job shop.

## Common Mistakes in MRP Software Selection

Based on 35 years of experience helping manufacturers choose and implement planning tools, these are the most frequent selection mistakes:

**1. Buying for features you will never use.** Enterprise ERP modules bundled with MRP sound impressive but add cost and complexity without value for small operations.

**2. Ignoring [data readiness](/blog/common-mrp-mistakes).** No MRP software can compensate for inaccurate BOMs or unreliable inventory counts. Budget time and resources for data preparation.

**3. Choosing based on price alone.** The cheapest option may lack critical features like finite capacity scheduling or [multi-level BOM](/blog/multi-level-bom-mrp) support that you will need as you grow.

**4. Not involving shop floor users.** Planners and shop floor supervisors must find the tool usable. Include them in the evaluation process.

**5. Underestimating the [spreadsheet to MRP transition](/blog/mrp-vs-spreadsheet).** Moving from Excel to MRP requires process changes, not just new software. Plan for change management.
