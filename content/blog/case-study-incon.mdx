## Why Labor Scheduling Matters More Than You Think

### Machines Are Fixed, Labor Is Flexible

Machines have a predictable capacity. They run at known speeds for known hours. Labor is variable — people call in sick, skills vary, cross-training creates options, overtime is possible but expensive. This variability makes labor scheduling more complex and more impactful than machine scheduling alone.

### The Promise Date Problem

When a customer asks "when can you deliver?" the answer depends not just on machine availability but on whether you have the right skilled workers available at the right time. Without [labor scheduling](/labor-scheduling-manufacturing), promise dates are based on machine capacity alone — which overpromises when labor is the constraint.

### Proactive Beats Reactive

Seeing labor needs in advance — weeks or months out — transforms workforce management from reactive to proactive. Instead of scrambling to find coverage for tomorrow's shortage, you can plan overtime, cross-training, or temporary staffing well in advance.

## Lessons for Electronics Manufacturers

**Schedule labor alongside machines.** If you schedule machines but not labor, you are only seeing half the capacity picture. [Resource Manager DB](/resource-manager-db-2) handles both in a single scheduling model.

**Integrate with what you have.** This manufacturer integrated RMDB with both Macola ERP and a custom labor system. The flexibility to work with existing systems — rather than requiring everything to be replaced — is essential for practical implementation.

**Let your ERP vendor guide you.** Exact Software recommended RMDB because they understood their own scheduling limitations. If your ERP vendor recommends a scheduling add-on, listen — they know where their system falls short.
