## Company Background

### Infor

Infor is a major enterprise software company (now owned by Koch Industries) serving over 65,000 customers across multiple industries. In manufacturing, Infor offers CloudSuite Industrial (formerly SyteLine), CloudSuite Automotive, CloudSuite Aerospace & Defense, and other industry-specific ERP platforms. The APS component provides finite capacity scheduling integrated with Infor's broader manufacturing operations suite including MES, supply chain management, quality, and analytics.

Infor's strength is industry-specific functionality. Rather than offering a generic ERP, Infor tailors its platforms to the specific requirements of automotive, aerospace, food and beverage, fashion, and other verticals.

### RMDB by User Solutions

[RMDB](/resource-manager-db-2) is developed by User Solutions, founded in 1991 with 35+ years in manufacturing scheduling. RMDB is a dedicated [finite capacity scheduling](/blogs/finite-capacity-planning-guide) system that works as an add-on to any ERP — including Infor platforms. It serves small to mid-size manufacturers across multiple industries.

## The Enterprise Platform Question

The core decision between Infor APS and RMDB often comes down to whether you need an enterprise platform or a focused scheduling tool.

### When You Need an Enterprise Platform

If your organization is implementing Infor CloudSuite for ERP, MES, supply chain, and quality — and scheduling is one module within that broader transformation — Infor APS is the natural choice. The native integration eliminates data mapping, provides a single vendor relationship, and ensures scheduling data flows seamlessly across the platform.

However, this approach comes with enterprise-scale cost, timeline, and complexity. Infor CloudSuite implementations are major organizational projects, not plug-and-play tool deployments.

### When You Need Better Scheduling

If your primary need is better scheduling — not a new ERP, not MES, not supply chain redesign — adding RMDB to your existing systems is faster, cheaper, and more focused. You keep what works (your current ERP, whatever it is) and add what is missing (finite capacity scheduling intelligence).

This is the [ERP scheduling add-on](/blogs/erp-scheduling-add-on-guide) approach, and it is how most small to mid-size manufacturers should address scheduling problems.

## Pricing: Enterprise vs Practical

### Infor CloudSuite (Including APS)

Infor CloudSuite pricing reflects enterprise software economics:
- **Annual subscription**: $100,000-$500,000+ depending on modules and user count
- **Implementation**: $150,000-$500,000+
- **First-year total**: $250,000-$1,000,000+
- **5-year total**: $650,000-$2,500,000+

These costs cover far more than scheduling — they include the full ERP, MES, analytics, and other modules. But if scheduling is your primary need, you are paying enterprise ERP prices for one capability.

## When Infor APS Is the Better Choice

Infor APS makes sense when:

- **You are implementing or already running Infor CloudSuite** and want integrated scheduling within your ERP ecosystem.
- **You are a large manufacturer** (500+ employees) with the budget and organizational readiness for an enterprise platform.
- **Industry-specific compliance** matters (automotive JIT, food safety, aerospace traceability) and Infor's vertical modules address those requirements.
- **Multi-plant scheduling** with supply chain synchronization is a core requirement.
- **You want AI-assisted scheduling** through Infor Coleman for large-scale optimization.

## When RMDB Is the Better Choice

RMDB is the stronger fit when:

- **You are a small to mid-size manufacturer** (10-500 employees) who needs scheduling without enterprise investment.
- **Scheduling is your primary problem**, not ERP replacement. RMDB adds scheduling intelligence to your existing [ERP system](/blogs/erp-scheduling-add-on-guide).
- **Budget is constrained.** RMDB costs less than 5% of a typical Infor CloudSuite deployment while delivering the scheduling capability most SMBs need.
- **You cannot wait 12-18 months.** RMDB's [5-day implementation](/blogs/scheduling-software-5-day-implementation) gets you live scheduling this week.
- **You value vendor independence.** RMDB works with any ERP — if you change systems later, your scheduling tool stays.
- **You run a [job shop](/blogs/what-is-job-shop-scheduling)** or make-to-order operation where RMDB's specialization aligns directly.

## The Bottom Line

Infor is a powerful enterprise platform that delivers comprehensive manufacturing operations management for large organizations. Its APS module is capable and benefits from deep integration with Infor's industry-specific ERP and MES offerings.

For the vast majority of small to mid-size manufacturers, Infor is over-scoped, over-priced, and over-complex for their scheduling needs. RMDB provides the [finite capacity scheduling](/blogs/finite-capacity-planning-guide), visual planning, what-if analysis, and constraint management these manufacturers need — deployed in days at a fraction of the cost, with no ecosystem lock-in.

The right tool depends on your organization's size, budget, and whether scheduling improvement requires an enterprise platform transformation or a focused addition to your existing systems.
