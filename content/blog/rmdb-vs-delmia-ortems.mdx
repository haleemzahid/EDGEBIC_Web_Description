**RMDB vs DELMIA Ortems** is a comparison between a focused scheduling tool for small to mid-size manufacturers and an enterprise APS platform backed by Dassault Systemes, one of the world's largest industrial software companies. Both deliver finite capacity scheduling, but they serve fundamentally different market segments with dramatically different cost structures and implementation approaches. This guide helps you determine which fits your operation.

For context across the full APS landscape, see our [production scheduling software comparison guide](/blog/production-scheduling-software-comparison).

## Company Background

### DELMIA Ortems

Ortems was founded in 1997 in France as a specialist APS vendor. The company built a strong reputation in European manufacturing, particularly in food and beverage, pharmaceutical, automotive, and consumer goods industries. Dassault Systemes acquired Ortems in 2014 and integrated it into the DELMIA portfolio — Dassault's manufacturing operations platform that also includes MES, manufacturing simulation, and supply chain planning.

DELMIA Ortems is now positioned as part of Dassault's 3DEXPERIENCE platform, connecting scheduling with product design (CATIA), PLM (ENOVIA), and simulation (SIMULIA). For manufacturers in the Dassault ecosystem, this integration creates a digital thread from design through production.

### RMDB by User Solutions

[RMDB](/resource-manager-db-2) is developed by User Solutions, founded in 1991 with 35+ years in manufacturing scheduling. RMDB is a dedicated [finite capacity scheduling](/blog/finite-capacity-planning-guide) system that works as an add-on to any ERP, serving small to mid-size manufacturers across aerospace, defense, automotive, electronics, and job shop machining.

## Scheduling Engine Comparison

Both RMDB and DELMIA Ortems provide genuine finite capacity scheduling — not the simplified capacity planning found in most ERP systems.

### DELMIA Ortems Strengths

DELMIA Ortems offers three tiers of scheduling capability:

- **Production Planner**: Basic scheduling and capacity visualization for initial planning
- **Production Scheduler**: Advanced finite capacity scheduling with constraints and optimization
- **Manufacturing Planner**: Multi-site planning with supply chain synchronization

The scheduler handles complex constraint networks, material pegging, multiple optimization criteria, and what-if analysis. Its particular strength is in process manufacturing environments (food, beverage, pharmaceutical, chemical) where batch sequencing, cleaning constraints, and shelf-life considerations add layers of complexity.

### RMDB Strengths

RMDB delivers advanced finite capacity scheduling with particular strength in discrete manufacturing:

- Multi-constraint optimization across machines, labor, tooling, and materials
- [EDGEBI visual Gantt](/edgebi) with drag-and-drop for real-time schedule adjustment
- What-if scenario analysis for testing schedule changes
- Sequence-dependent setup optimization
- [Job shop](/blog/what-is-job-shop-scheduling) and make-to-order specialization
- [5-day implementation](/blog/scheduling-software-5-day-implementation) with real production data

For discrete [manufacturers](/blog/manufacturing-scheduling-by-industry) — job shops, machine shops, fabricators, assembly operations — RMDB's scheduling engine handles the same complexity at a fraction of the cost.

## The Dassault Ecosystem Factor

The biggest differentiator for DELMIA Ortems is not the scheduling engine itself — it is the Dassault Systemes ecosystem.

For manufacturers already invested in CATIA for design, ENOVIA for PLM, and SIMULIA for simulation, DELMIA Ortems creates a unified digital thread where product changes flow automatically from design through scheduling. This integration is powerful for large enterprises where design-to-manufacturing synchronization is a competitive advantage.

For manufacturers who are not Dassault shops — which includes the vast majority of small to mid-size manufacturers — this ecosystem integration adds no value while adding significant cost. RMDB's vendor-agnostic approach works with whatever systems you already have, whether that is SAP, Epicor, [JobBOSS](/blog/rmdb-vs-jobboss), Sage, or any other platform.

## Pricing: Enterprise vs Practical

### DELMIA Ortems Pricing

As part of the Dassault 3DEXPERIENCE platform, Ortems pricing reflects enterprise software economics:
- **Software**: $100,000-$300,000+ depending on modules and user count
- **Implementation**: $75,000-$250,000+
- **Annual maintenance/subscription**: Significant recurring costs
- **Total first-year**: $175,000-$550,000+

### RMDB Pricing

- **One-time license**: $5,000-$15,000
- **Implementation**: Included in 5-day program
- **Optional maintenance**: Fraction of license cost
- **Total first-year**: $5,000-$15,000

The cost differential is 10-30x. For small to mid-size manufacturers, this is not a negotiable difference — it is the difference between feasible and not.

## When DELMIA Ortems Is the Better Choice

DELMIA Ortems makes sense when:

- **You are a large enterprise** (500+ employees) with significant scheduling complexity across multiple plants.
- **You are invested in the Dassault ecosystem** and want design-to-manufacturing digital thread integration.
- **You operate in process manufacturing** (food, pharma, chemical) where Ortems' batch scheduling and constraint handling excels.
- **You have the budget and timeline** for a 6-18 month enterprise APS deployment.
- **Supply chain planning** across facilities is a core requirement.

## When RMDB Is the Better Choice

RMDB is the stronger fit when:

- **You are a small to mid-size manufacturer** who needs enterprise-grade scheduling without enterprise-grade costs.
- **Scheduling is your primary need.** You do not need PLM integration, 3D simulation, or multi-plant supply chain planning — you need your jobs scheduled correctly and on time.
- **Budget is constrained.** At 90%+ less than DELMIA Ortems, RMDB makes [advanced scheduling](/blog/best-production-scheduling-software) accessible.
- **Time is critical.** Five days to live scheduling vs 6-18 months is decisive when delivery problems are costing customers now.
- **You are not a Dassault shop.** If your CAD is SolidWorks or Autodesk, your ERP is Epicor or SAP, and your PLM is Teamcenter, the DELMIA ecosystem adds complexity, not value.
- **You run discrete manufacturing** — [job shops](/blog/job-shop-scheduling-challenges), machine shops, fabrication, assembly — where RMDB's specialization aligns perfectly.

## The Bottom Line

DELMIA Ortems is a world-class APS platform with deep roots in European manufacturing and strong integration within the Dassault ecosystem. For large enterprises pursuing digital manufacturing transformation within the Dassault technology stack, it delivers genuine value.

For the vast majority of small to mid-size manufacturers, DELMIA Ortems is dramatically over-scoped and over-priced. RMDB delivers the [finite capacity scheduling](/blog/finite-capacity-planning-guide), visual planning, what-if analysis, and constraint management these manufacturers need — in a week, at a fraction of the cost, without ecosystem dependencies.
