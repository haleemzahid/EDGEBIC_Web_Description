## The Challenge: Two Countries, Zero Effective Scheduling

Sleepmaster faced two distinct scheduling challenges simultaneously.

**Australia**: An outdated MRP system with no effective scheduling ability and cumbersome product costing. The system existed but was not meeting their needs.

**China**: A brand-new facility with no MRP system at all, staffed by people with little modern manufacturing experience. Everything needed to be built from scratch.

The problems Sleepmaster needed to solve:
- Scheduling that allows quick changes in very dynamic demand situations
- Quick and easy product costing
- Materials requirements planning
- BOMs and routes that could be built quickly "on the run"

---

## The Solution: Phased, Affordable, International

Sleepmaster evaluated multiple systems — both local Australian solutions and global platforms. Most were "far more expensive and comprehensive" than what they needed. Resource Manager won on five criteria:

1. **Easy and intuitive to use** — no complex training required
2. **Usable in sections within days** — materials planning first, then costing, then scheduling
3. **Low cost** — affordable for a $50M organization without enterprise budgets
4. **Easily configurable and flexible** — adapts to their specific workflows
5. **Good remote support** — via Skype, phone, and GoToMeeting with fast turnaround

### The Phased Approach

Sleepmaster's implementation approach is a model for other [small manufacturers](/mrp-software-small-manufacturers):

**Phase 1: Materials planning** — Get visibility into what materials are needed, when, and how much. This delivers immediate value for purchasing.

**Phase 2: Costing** — Understand the true cost of each product based on BOMs, routings, and resource consumption. This supports pricing decisions and profitability analysis.

**Phase 3: Scheduling** — Add [finite capacity scheduling](/finite-capacity-scheduling-software) to optimize production flow and provide accurate delivery dates.

Each phase delivered value independently while building toward a complete manufacturing management system. This is the key advantage: "A whole system can be built up while it is generating value."

### China First

Sleepmaster strategically deployed Resource Manager in China first — the facility with no existing systems and limited manufacturing experience. This was the highest-impact starting point because it transformed a greenfield operation with zero planning capability into a structured manufacturing environment with materials planning, costing, and scheduling.

## Why Affordability Changes the Equation

### The Enterprise Software Tax

Most MRP and scheduling systems are priced for large enterprises. The license costs, implementation fees, and ongoing maintenance create a "software tax" that small manufacturers cannot justify. Sleepmaster explicitly noted that most alternatives were "far more expensive and comprehensive" — meaning they were paying for capabilities they would never use.

### Speed to Value Matters More Than Feature Depth

For a small manufacturer, the ability to start generating value within days matters more than having every conceivable feature. Sleepmaster started using materials planning immediately, then layered in costing and scheduling over time. This approach generates ROI from day one rather than waiting months for a comprehensive implementation to complete.

### International Operations Need Simple Tools

When deploying across countries — especially in facilities with limited manufacturing experience — simplicity is critical. Complex enterprise systems that require specialized training and dedicated IT support are impractical for international startup operations. Resource Manager's simplicity and remote support capability made international deployment feasible.

---

## Lessons for Small and International Manufacturers

**Phase your implementation.** Do not try to implement everything at once. Start with the area that delivers the most immediate value — usually materials planning or scheduling — and expand from there.

**Evaluate total cost, not just license cost.** Consider implementation time, training requirements, ongoing maintenance, and support costs. A "cheaper" system that takes three times as long to implement may cost more overall.

**Deploy to your biggest pain point first.** Sleepmaster deployed to China first because that was where the need was greatest. Focus your implementation where it will have the most impact.

**Simplicity is a feature.** For small manufacturers and international operations, simplicity of use is more valuable than comprehensive feature lists. If your team cannot use the system easily, features do not matter.
