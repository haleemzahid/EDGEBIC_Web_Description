[Finite capacity scheduling](/finite-capacity-scheduling-software) is not just for manufacturing. **GEMS Healthcare Solutions**, a wholly-owned subsidiary of GE Medical Systems headquartered in San Ramon, California, proved that the same scheduling principles that optimize factory floors can transform professional services delivery.

GEMS provides integrated data mining, internet-based benchmarking, and consulting solutions to healthcare providers. Their biggest scheduling challenge: sharing the same financial analysts across multiple consulting projects with different deadlines and different clients.

Read the [full GEMS Healthcare success story](/success-stories/finite-capacity-resource-scheduling-for-consulting-projects).

---

## The Challenge: Shared Resources, Multiple Projects, Unreliable Delivery

Shavonna Portue, Director of Product Operations, described the core problem: "Delivering accurate information to our customers in a timely manner is the core of our business. Each financial analyst on our staff handles multiple accounts, and our biggest challenge was finding a resource scheduling system which would allow us to share the same resources across multiple projects."

### Failed Alternatives

Before finding User Solutions, GEMS tried several scheduling options including Microsoft Project. The alternatives were "difficult to maintain in our environment, or simply undependable." The result was predictable: "We were unable to adequately predict delivery times and our customers were suffering."

This mirrors what manufacturers experience when they try to use project management tools for production scheduling. Project management software assumes dedicated resources; [production scheduling software](/production-scheduling-software) handles shared resources across competing demands — which is the reality in both manufacturing and professional services.

---

## The Solution: Scheduling Meets Customer Expectations

GEMS implemented Workcenter SchedulerXL and gained immediate operational improvements.

Shavonna reported the key benefits: "Setting accurate customer expectations has been the greatest benefit. We are able to track problem areas and get good feedback on potential bottlenecks before they happen. No more unknown statuses, and it makes adjusting schedules easy! We now know exactly what staffing requirements are and can flex our workforce to fit the changing needs of our customers."

### Breaking Down the Benefits

**Accurate expectations** — Clients received reliable delivery commitments based on actual analyst availability, not optimistic guessing.

**Bottleneck prevention** — The system showed when analyst workloads would peak, enabling proactive reassignment before deadlines were missed.

**Status visibility** — No more "unknown statuses" — project managers could see exactly where each engagement stood.

**Workforce flexibility** — Understanding staffing requirements enabled GEMS to flex their workforce with changing demands.

## Why This Matters Beyond Manufacturing

The GEMS Healthcare case study demonstrates that finite capacity scheduling principles are universal. Any organization that shares skilled resources across multiple projects faces the same fundamental scheduling problem:

- **Limited resources** (analysts, engineers, technicians) must be allocated across competing demands
- **Delivery commitments** depend on resource availability, not just task duration
- **Bottlenecks** emerge when multiple projects need the same person simultaneously
- **Visibility** into future resource loading enables proactive management

Whether those resources are CNC machines in a [job shop](/scheduling-software-for-job-shops) or financial analysts in a consulting firm, the scheduling math is identical. The tools that solve one problem solve both.

---

## Lessons for Professional Services and Manufacturing

**Shared resources require finite capacity scheduling.** If your people or machines serve multiple projects or customers simultaneously, infinite capacity assumptions will produce unreliable schedules. Only [finite capacity scheduling](/finite-capacity-scheduling-software) respects the reality that resources cannot be double-booked.

**Customer satisfaction starts with accurate scheduling.** GEMS identified "setting accurate customer expectations" as the greatest benefit. This applies equally to manufacturers: the most impactful thing you can do for customer satisfaction is deliver what you promise, when you promise it.

**Try dedicated scheduling tools before building custom solutions.** GEMS tried Microsoft Project and other tools before finding the right fit. Many organizations waste time trying to force general-purpose tools into scheduling roles they were not designed for.
