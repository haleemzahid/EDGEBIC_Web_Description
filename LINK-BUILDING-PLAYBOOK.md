## CHANNEL 2: HARO / CONNECTIVELY / QWOTED

### Setup
1. Sign up at https://www.connectively.us (formerly HARO)
2. Sign up at https://qwoted.com
3. Set alerts for: "manufacturing", "production scheduling", "ERP", "supply chain", "factory", "Industry 4.0", "lean manufacturing"

### Response Template

```
SOURCE: [Publication Name]
QUERY: [Their question]

RESPONSE:

[Your Name]: Haleem Zahid, CEO of User Solutions Inc. (usersolutions.com)

[2-3 paragraph response answering their specific question]

Key credentials:
- CEO of User Solutions, manufacturing scheduling software company (est. 1991)
- 35+ years building scheduling tools for manufacturers
- Clients include GE, Cummins, BAE Systems, and the US Navy
- Implemented scheduling in 100+ factories across 20+ industries

[Optional: 1-2 sentence quote they can pull directly]

Contact: [email]
Website: https://usersolutions.com
LinkedIn: [LinkedIn URL]
```

### HARO Response Rules
- **Respond within 2 hours** of query publication (speed wins)
- **Lead with the answer**, not your credentials
- **Include a quotable soundbite** (journalists love ready-to-use quotes)
- **Be specific** — use numbers, not vague claims ("15-25% lead time reduction" not "significant improvement")
- **Don't pitch your product** — answer the question, let the bio do the selling

### Sample HARO Responses

**Query**: "Looking for manufacturing experts to comment on supply chain challenges in 2026"

```
The biggest scheduling challenge manufacturers face in 2026 isn't supply 
chain disruption itself — it's the inability to reschedule in real-time 
when disruptions happen.

Most manufacturers still use spreadsheets or basic ERP modules that can't 
recalculate a production schedule when a critical shipment is delayed. The 
result? Three days of manual replanning that should take three minutes.

In our experience implementing scheduling software in 100+ factories, 
manufacturers who switch from spreadsheet scheduling to finite capacity 
scheduling reduce their disruption recovery time from 2-3 days to under 
4 hours.

— Haleem Zahid, CEO, User Solutions Inc. (35 years in manufacturing software)
```

**Query**: "What KPIs should small manufacturers track?"

```
Small manufacturers should focus on just five KPIs — tracking more creates 
dashboard fatigue without improving decisions:

1. On-Time Delivery Rate (target: >95%)
2. Schedule Adherence (target: >90%)
3. Overall Equipment Effectiveness / OEE (target: >65% for job shops)
4. Manufacturing Lead Time (benchmark against your top 3 competitors)
5. WIP Inventory Turns (target: 12+ per year)

The most overlooked metric is schedule adherence — it tells you whether 
your production plan is realistic. If you're consistently below 80%, 
your scheduling method is broken, not your shop floor.

— Haleem Zahid, CEO, User Solutions Inc.
```
